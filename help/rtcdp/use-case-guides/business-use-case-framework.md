# Leverage customer experience use cases to drive business goals

Move from one-off use cases to scalable, repeatable customer experiences. Use this framework to define, prioritize, and activate use cases tied to measurable business outcomes. It provides a shared language across marketing, product, technology, analytics, and creative—so teams move from idea to execution aligned on a common goal.

## Use case framework: Why → Who / What / Where → How

- **Why:** The business objective and north star KPI. *The why grounds everything else.*  
- **Who:** Defined audience or segment  
- **What:** Message, offer, or call to action  
- **Where:** Channel or touchpoint  
- **How:** Execution across five pillars — data, audience, journey, measurement, and content  

This structure ensures teams work from the same script and stay aligned as complexity increases.

## From idea to impact

- **Ideate:** Define use cases in a standard format (why → who/what/where → how) in a cross-functional session  
- **Prioritize:** Use a value vs. feasibility model as a value compass—not a precise ROI model—and document a testable hypothesis  
- **Plan & Activate:** Define requirements and measurement upfront, then build and launch (data, audiences, journeys, content, QA)  
- **Measure:** Evaluate performance against KPIs using test-and-control to verify impact  
- **Scale:** Refine and expand based on results; maintain a prioritized, cross-functional roadmap (not a wish list)  

## Evolve your use cases (Crawl → Walk → Run)

- **Crawl:** Simple, batched, channel-specific use cases using limited data  
- **Walk:** Triggered, cross-channel journeys with more integrated data and measurement  
- **Run:** Real-time, individualized experiences powered by unified profiles and advanced decisioning  

You don't need to start at "run" to deliver value—focus on the next step based on your current capabilities.

## How to apply

- **Inputs:** Business goal (why), KPI, available data, channels, cross-functional stakeholders  
- **Outputs:** Standardized use case definition, testable hypothesis, prioritized roadmap, activation and measurement plan
